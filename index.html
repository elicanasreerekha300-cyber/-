The Art of Silk Wrapping

Originating from the Ming Dynasty, "Chan Hua" (Silk Wrapping Flowers) is a craft of patience and precision.

Master Li, a 78-year-old inheritor from Hubei, describes the process as "painting with silk threads." Every petal requires thousands of wraps, maintaining consistent tension to ensure the light reflects perfectly off the silk surface.

Through our digital twin technology, we capture the microscopic details of her work, preserving not just the object, but the hand movements and spirit of the artisan. This ancient craft has been passed down through generations, surviving changing dynasties and modern industrialization.
